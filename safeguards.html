Public-health layer

Safeguards

TwinBridge treats safeguards as the public-health layer that makes synthetic controls decision-ready rather than merely plausible.

Gate 1 — Overlap / positivity

Quantify covariate shift and identify “no-support” strata where twins are invalid.

Gate 2 — Fidelity in controls

Calibration-in-the-large and slope, Brier/Brier skill, survival calibration where relevant, plus subgroup reliability.

Gate 3 — Causal plausibility

Negative-control outcomes, sensitivity to residual confounding, and assessment of time-varying care feedback—then scope claims accordingly.

Decision output

We translate bias + variance into a practical “how much control-arm burden can be reduced without losing decision quality” summary (e.g., an effective control size framing).
